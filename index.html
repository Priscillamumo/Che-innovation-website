NeoNest

NeoNest is a low-cost infant warmer designed to prevent preterm hypothermia during ambulance trips in low- and middle-income countries. Developed by Duke and Makerere Universities, it provides a reliable, portable, and affordable solution.

NeoNest was developed during the Duke-Makerere Biomedical Engineering Fellowship, transforming an old shipping container into a makerspace for innovation.

NeoNest is the first infant warmer designed for local ambulances, maintaining temperature for preterm infants during transport to national referral hospitals.

The Problem

Every 40 seconds, a premature baby dies, with 99% of these deaths occurring in low- and middle-income countries. In Kampala, at Kawempe National Referral Hospital (KNRH), one of Uganda’s largest referral centers, 40% of babies born and received are premature. These babies often arrive from rural villages 2-4 hours away, and during these long trips, there are no effective methods to keep them warm.

Current solutions, such as placing gloves with warm water around the baby, are inefficient and insufficient. The standard of care in the United States for transport incubators costs well over $5,000 USD, making it inaccessible for many low-income countries.

Why This Matters

Preterm infants are highly vulnerable to hypothermia, which can lead to severe complications and even death. Addressing this issue with cost-effective solutions can significantly improve survival rates and health outcomes for these infants.

Our Solution

Stable Temperature

NeoNest maintains a consistent temperature between 36°C to 37°C for up to 4 hours, ensuring that preterm infants remain warm and safe during transport. This stable temperature is crucial for preventing hypothermia and improving survival rates.

Portability

Our device is portable and lightweight, weighing less than 3 kg. This makes it easy to transport and handle, especially in emergency situations, ensuring that it can be quickly and efficiently deployed where it's needed most.

Cost-Effective

With materials costing under $75, NeoNest is a cost-effective solution designed to be affordable for low- and middle-income countries. This cost efficiency ensures wider accessibility and deployment in resource-limited settings.

Adjustability

NeoNest is adjustable to fit various infant sizes, providing a secure and comfortable environment for preterm babies. The adjustable design ensures that the warmer can be used for a wide range of infants, enhancing its versatility.

Safety

Designed for use in ambulances, NeoNest is safe and secure, ensuring that preterm infants are protected during transport. The device includes safety features that prevent overheating and ensure a stable, controlled environment.

Our Impact

Current Use

Currently a standalone component of our design, the nest is already being used at Kawempe National Referral Hospital for securing babies in the infant warmers.

Positive Outcomes

Our innovation is making a significant impact by providing a reliable, portable, and affordable solution for preterm infants, helping to prevent hypothermia during ambulance trips. This is improving survival rates and health outcomes for many infants in low- and middle-income countries.
